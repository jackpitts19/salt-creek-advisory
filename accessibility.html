Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to making this site usable for every visitor, and how to reach us if something is not working for you.

Last updated: July 5, 2026

Salt Creek Advisory LLC is committed to making saltcreekadvisory.com accessible to as many people as possible, including visitors who use assistive technology such as screen readers, screen magnification, voice recognition software, or keyboard-only navigation.

Our Standard

We aim to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA, which are widely recognized standards for making web content usable by people with disabilities. This includes efforts such as:

  • Semantic HTML structure and heading hierarchy so screen readers can navigate the page logically;
  • Descriptive alternative text for meaningful images;
  • Sufficient color contrast between text and background;
  • Keyboard-operable navigation, menus, and interactive elements; and
  • Legible type sizes and clear, plain-language content.

Ongoing Work

Accessibility is an ongoing effort, not a one-time project. This Site is built and maintained on an ongoing basis, and we review and improve it as we become aware of areas that fall short of our standard. Despite our efforts, some parts of the Site may not yet be fully accessible, particularly interactive elements like the valuation tool, which we continue to refine.

Third-Party Content

Some content on the Site, such as our embedded scheduling tool or linked podcast platforms, is hosted by third parties. We select vendors that are broadly attentive to accessibility, but we do not control their code and cannot guarantee full compliance with the standard above.

Feedback

If you encounter a barrier using this Site, or have suggestions for how we can improve accessibility, please contact us at jack@saltcreekadvisory.com or connor@saltcreekadvisory.com. Please describe the page and the issue you encountered, and we will work to address it and respond to you.

See also our Privacy Policy and Terms of Use.

Salt Creek Advisory LLC operates as an investment bank in the traditional lower middle market sense: we advise privately held business owners on the sale and acquisition of their companies. We are not a chartered bank or depository institution, we do not accept deposits, and no funds held in connection with an engagement are FDIC or NCUA insured. We are not a broker-dealer registered with FINRA or the SEC and are not a registered investment adviser. To the extent applicable, our M&A engagements are conducted in reliance on the M&A broker exemption at Section 15(b)(13) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (15 U.S.C. § 78o(b)(13)), under which “an M&A broker shall be exempt from registration” when acting solely in connection with the transfer of ownership of an “eligible privately held company,” defined as a company with no registered securities whose EBITDA is less than $25,000,000 or whose gross revenues are less than $250,000,000. That exemption is unavailable to any broker that holds client funds or securities, provides transaction financing, forms or represents a group of buyers, deals with a passive buyer, or represents both sides of a transaction without written disclosure and consent, and we do not engage in any of those activities. We hold or obtain state business broker licensure where required, and where a transaction calls for services only a licensed broker-dealer may perform, those services are referred to and executed through a properly registered broker-dealer. We do not hold, custody, or take possession of client funds or securities at any time. All engagements are advisory in nature; nothing on this site is an offer or solicitation to buy or sell any security, and nothing here is legal, tax, or investment advice. Consult your own qualified advisors.
